Factory pollution also known as industrial pollution
is the contamination of the environment caused by various manufacturing
processes and activities carried out in factories and industrial facilities.
These operations often release harmful substances, waste materials,
and pollutants into the air, water, and soil, leading to adverse effects on
human health, wildlife, and ecosystem

As the global demand for products and goods continues to rise,
the industrial sector has expanded significantly, contributing to
economic growth and technological advancements. However,
this rapid industrialization has come at a cost to the environment,
as factories release substantial amounts of pollutants
into the surrounding environment

About


Geographic Information Systems (gis) are powerful tools that can be utilized effectively to monitor and follow up on pollution in various environments. GIS combines spatial data, such as maps and satellite imagery, with attribute data, such as pollution levels, sources, and other relevant information. This integration allows for a comprehensive understanding of pollution patterns and trends, aiding in effective pollution management and decision-making
